About Me

As I mentioned above, I am a queer person that enjoys working for and with queer organizations. My journey began while attending UC Berkeley, where I got my degree in Molecular and Cell Biology. I worked in labs for a while but found the lack of community disheartening. I moved to San Francisco and made my way through the tech field. A lot of my early work was with education and tech, but I quickly realized that I needed to be on the learner side! Insert Thinkful! With their help I launched my developer career and used my powers for the good of the people.

As I made my career a reality I started to see a huge gap in access to resources for queer orgs. Mainly, a lot of essential queer orgs had underdeveloped and underwhelming web pages. I saw an opportunity to give back, improve, automate, and bring forward their presence. I'm proud to know that I was able to serve the following communities:

  • Underrepresented groups of color
  • LGBTQ+
  • Sex workers

I do want to clarify that these are not the only communities I work with. However, these communities are largely underserved, underfunded and are not shown compassion. I have the opportunity to bridge them to the current forms of communication that improve outreach and productivity. If you're a developer, I hope you consider doing the same. If you're looking for a developer, then I hope you have found what you're looking for.
